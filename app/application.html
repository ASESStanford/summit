Let's break down the application process.

As much as we'd love to, we can't grant everyone an interview – there's simply too many of you. Every Summit for the past 17 years, we've worked hard on improving our application process – we aim for simplicity, authenticity, and fairness.

Some application tips.

Tip 1

Be yourself. Duh! But you’d be surprised by how many generic applications we receive every year. Don’t tell us what you think we want to hear; being genuine and real goes a long, long way. If you’re a passionate person, show your passion; if you’re a funny person, give us your best joke; if you’re a weird individual, show us the weird, we love weird! ...Just not too weird…

Tip 2

Put in the time. Really think through these questions and make your responses meaningful. Longer does not mean better – we value insightful responses much more than lengthy responses. Note: It may be a good idea to write your responses in a separate document and then transfer them over to this application form.

Tip 3

Have fun. We get it. At the end of the day, you’re applying against the world’s brightest entrepreneurs for one of 35 spots to learn from the best of the best at Stanford University. But there’s nothing worse than reading an application that’s just a resume in paragraph form. Have fun with the responses and show your personality and creativity!
